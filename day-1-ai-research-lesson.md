## Timeplan (60 minutes)

## 0-10 min: Warm-up and framing

- Read the core project context aloud from [gpt-context.md](/home/kevin/coding/austin/gpt-context.md) “One-Sentence Pitch” and “Core Idea.”
- Define a one-sentence project question:
  - Example: How can we explain AI’s environmental and social benefits and costs in a balanced, student-friendly way?
- Write this in the notebook as the day anchor.

## 10-20 min: Choose scope for Day 1

- Use [day-1.md](/home/kevin/coding/austin/day-1.md) Step 2 and pick **one** audience only:
  - Middle/high school students
  - Selective audience, teachers, and parents as supporting examples
- Decide one sentence for audience:
  - “This site is for high school students learning AI and sustainability.”
- Name the 3 pages that must exist by end of Day 1:
  - Homepage
  - Statistics Dashboard
  - Sources

## 20-35 min: Research map from existing anchors

- Use Step 4 and Research Categories in [day-1.md](/home/kevin/coding/austin/day-1.md) as the map:
  - AI in software engineering
  - Environmental costs
  - Economic impact
  - Future solutions
- Add these columns to a spreadsheet or notes:
  - Topic
  - Claim/Finding
  - Why it matters to students
  - Source name
  - Source URL
  - Year
  - Confidence (High, Medium, Low)
  - Notes (uncertainty / limits)
- Rule: no uncited numbers and no fake precision.

## 35-50 min: AI research sprint

- Start with the anchor source section in [gpt-context.md](/home/kevin/coding/austin/gpt-context.md) “Key Source Anchors To Research And Cite.”
- Capture at least one source from each anchor:
  - AI energy and data centers
  - U.S. data center energy growth
  - Data center water use
  - Neuromorphic or brain-inspired work
  - At least one methodology/caution source for uncertainty
- For each source, capture exactly:
  - The claim you plan to use
  - The year and number/estimate
  - The sentence on limits, e.g. “depends on model size, hardware, location, grid mix.”

## 50-60 min: Convert research to site content plan

- Open [curriculum.md](/home/kevin/coding/austin/curriculum.md) Week 1 and map 4 content blocks:
  - Hero message
  - One benefit section
  - One cost section
  - One source card
- Fill one-page “Research-to-page map”:
  - Which source supports each block
  - Where it appears on the homepage or future page
- Decide the tiny Day 1 win:
  - A research tracker with at least 6 claims and 6 links
  - A paper sketch of homepage sections
  - A one-line source policy rule

## AI research workflow checklist

- Confirm the claim is from a credible source.
- Check if the same idea appears in at least two places.
- Capture both a numeric value and a context note.
- Mark every claim as “supported,” “estimated,” or “uncertain.”
- Never copy unsupported claims from social posts, videos, or summaries.
- Add source citations in plain language:
  - What is said
  - Where it came from
  - Why this claim is not universal

## Exit ticket (2 minutes)

Each student answers:

- What is our exact Day 1 question?
- Which 3 sources will we trust first?
- Which 2 facts are strong enough to put on the first page now?
- What is one uncertainty to flag on the site?
